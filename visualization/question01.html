research question

How is the Internet companies developed globally for recent ten years?








Description Protocol Data

Description

From a global view, the high-traffic website concentrated on the United States and China, followed by Russia and Japan. Europe, the Middle East, Canada, Australia, and other regions showed a weaker Internet developing state. Besides, the companies with the highest traffic (such as Google, Facebook, Youtube) maintained the leading status, and the companies with the lower traffic were relatively volatile, which indicated a form of monopoly in the Internet world.

In details, there were 15 domain sites based on Google, such as google.de and google.fr. Google did occupy an important position in the Internet world. Additionally, the traffic data of several websites in the porn industry was growing. Ranking by country, despite that the top websites in China and the U.S. were in a stable condition, causing a huge traffic pressure, there were other websites constantly could find an opportunity to develop and grow.

Protocol

Through visiting the Wikipedia editing history, the data of Alexa Internet ranking (Lists of most popular websites) was manually collected, which filtered the top 50 websites in each duration from 2012 to 2019, including the elements of the site, the site country, the site type, and the ranking. Then, through Tableau Prep Builder, one integrated single CSV file was obtained. Because only the top 50 ranking websites were collected in each duration, there existed some missing data, which means the ranking of these websites exceeded the top 50. For rough estimation for the average ranking, the missing data were replaced by number 60 to calculate.

Then the final dataset file was imported into Data Illustrator to generate a matrix diagram, Raw Graphs to generate a flow diagram, and then was manually adjusted in Adobe Illustrator to get the final visualization of site and country. Finally, the SVG files were exported and combined with the D3.js Library to get the interactive version.
